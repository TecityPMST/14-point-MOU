State of the MOU
On Day 14 of 60, the ceasefire is holding for now but the negotiation track has slipped. The 29 Jun stand-down held into a second day with no new strikes and a US official saying Washington is "holding up our end" — yet Article 1 stays in breach territory after the 25–28 Jun strike-and-retaliation cycle, and Israel's warning that war could resume within 48 hours still hangs over the pause. Diplomacy narrowed in Doha: Witkoff and Kushner met mediators, but Qatar said no high-level US–Iran meeting was taking place, Iran refused direct talks and routed contacts through Qatari mediators, and Tehran made implementation of the interim terms — above all the frozen-asset release — a precondition for continuing (Art 3, contested). Frozen funds (Art 11) became the day's flashpoint: Pezeshkian announced $6B of $12B held in Qatar would be released for humanitarian goods, while US and Qatari officials said nothing had actually been freed. Hormuz (Art 5) stayed unresolved — Iran rejected any third-party role in demining and an Omani service-fee proposal clashed with Oman's own no-tolls stance. Oil waivers (Art 10) remain the one clearly delivered item, in force through 21 Aug.
